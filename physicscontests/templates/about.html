Why contests?

Learning through contests is a very effective and funny way of learning. I experienced that in the field of computer science, where you can find a ton of coding competitions. This site does actually the same for physics. The problems you will encounter here do not focus on querying your knowledge, but rather challenge your problem solving skills.

  • Improve your problem solving skills
  • Improve your physics skills
  • Have fun competing with others
  • Win competitions and increase your rating!
Let's start

Rules

  • Normally, you should only participate on your own, but since this is kind of a demo-test-version, teamwork is ok.
  • Please do not hack the website or the server, I know that it is not secure yet.
  • Do not publish results until the end of the contest.
  • You will need to enter a number as an answer for each task. Your answer will be judged as correct if the relative error is less or equal to one hundredth. (Make sure to write decimal numbers with a dot, not a comma)
  • You are allowed to look up some mathematical definitions and physical constants (eg. on the Internet)
  • Please do not brute-force the solution, do not solve equations numerically (except if it is explicitly said in the statement), and do not look up similar problems.

Have much fun and good luck!
